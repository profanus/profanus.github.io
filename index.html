Real life comes first

We love World of Warcraft, but we have families, friends and jobs too. We don't play every day, and we don't expect you to either.

If you are looking for a guild that understands and encourages your life outside the game, we are are here for you.

Fun and progress

We believe fun and progress are not mutually exclusive. Our raid environment is relaxed and enjoyable, but we are serious about progression and fixing our mistakes.

Outside of raids, we organize many guild events, such as old raid achievement runs, PvP groups, mythic dungeon groups and world quest groups.

Raiding with Profanus

Profanus is a raiding guild on Ragnaros EU. Our purpose is to have good progression, while only raiding twice a week. In order to facilitate this, we have some guidelines and rules that apply to our raiders.

As a raiding guild, our primary goal is to kill as many bosses as possible per raid. We have outlined how we achieve this below. Every Profanus raider is expected to know and to follow these rules.

Raid times

We raid Wednesday and Monday from 20:00 server time until 23:00 server time.

During this time, we expect people to be online and available. We have a break in the middle of the raid where raiders can take care of whatever business.

Time inside raids

Our most important rule is not to waste time in raids. When properly followed, it ensures we can make the most of our raids. Below are some examples of how this rule applies in a concrete way, but the main takeaway is to be mindful of our time and not to waste it.

Preparation

Raiders should have enough food, flasks, and potions on you to make it through the raid. They are expected to be at the raid entrance 15 minutes before the raid. We expect them to have read up on the tactics of new bosses we will be progressing on and to know how to play their class and spec.

Fun and progress

Raids are fun, but they must also be serious. Jokes are only funny if they don't waste other people's time or effort. We like jokes, but during bosses we expect our raiders to pay attention to the fight, the raid leader and their role.

Attendance

We aim at having a raid team with at least 80% attendance. Since we only raid twice a week, it should be possible for everyone to meet this schedule.

If people cannot make a raid day, we expect them to the officers know well in advance and to decline the raid invite. If someone often cancels right before the raid or doesn't show, we will try to replace them to ensure our regular raiding schedule.

Loot

We have a loot council consisting of the officers and raid leader to distribute loot. They will attempt to distribute loot to increase the overall performance of the raid. This means raiders might not always get the loot they want when they want it.

When asked, the officers will provide a reason for the way they distribute loot. The goal is to be fair and sensible. At the end of the day, everyone will be geared in due time and there have generally never been issues regarding loot distribution in the raids.

Code of Conduct

We are committed to maintaining a peaceful, comfortable and tolerant community with people we consider friends and 'family'. As a member you are expected to have read, understood and abide by the following. Potential members are required to read this before applying. As we are an adult (20+) guild, the /g will sometimes contain adult content. Even so, these rules still apply. Minor offences will receive a warning, major and repeat offences will be punished with removal from the guild.

  • You are expected to treat fellow members in a friendly and inclusive manner. You are expected to treat constructive criticism with grace and dignity and be respectful of differing opinions.
  • You are required to respect the time and effort of your fellow guildies.
  • We operate a zero-tolerance policy towards slander and discrimination related to gender identity, body-size, race, ethnicity, nationality, sexuality, religious beliefs and level of experience. This also includes cruelty, derogatory commentary, bullying and sexual harassment.
  • While we are all intelligent people who enjoy a good debate, controversial topics such as politics and immigration should be kept to an absolute minimum and if possible out of the public chat / teamspeak channel.
  • If you have access to a player's personal information, you may not publish it without the player's explicit permission. This includes photos and anything related to social networks. You are not to beg players for boosts, gold, loot or items. We aim to help members to the best of our ability, but it is a privilege and should not be demanded.
  • The items in the guild bank are not a source of your gold. You are welcome to deposit items to contribute, however the officer responsible for the guild bank will decide whether it is necessary to sell an item and deposit profits in the bank.
  • Raid loot is viewed as a group commodity in Profanus and not a personal reward. Items that are distributed via loot council are given fairly to ensure that the raid team has the best chance of progressing further. You may inquire the reasons why an item went to a certain player, but you may not complain about not getting an item.
  • During raids we do not allow calling members out in public on the mistakes they make. Noticing these mistakes is the responsibility of the raid leader and role leaders. If you want to point something out, do it in /w to the raid leader. You may also offer help to the player in question provided that it is done in a friendly manner.
  • If you have an issue with any of the members, you are expected to attempt to resolve the issue with said member. If that is not productive, you may request assistance from an officer and the officer is obliged to assist you to the best of his/her ability.

Should any revisions be added to this, members will be notified accordingly.
